Sulfur Cosmos

Sulfur Cosmos, also known as Cosmos sulphureus, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family. It is native to Mexico, Central America, and northern South America, but it has been introduced into the United States, Europe, Asia, and Australia. The flowers are known for their bright yellow, orange, or red hues, making them a popular choice for gardens.

Sulfur Cosmos

Characteristics

Family: Asteraceae

Genus: Cosmos

Height: 1–6 ft

Flowering season: Summer to autumn

Significance

Sulfur Cosmos are appreciated for their vibrant colors and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Care Tips

Plant in full sun to partial shade.

Prefer well-drained soil.

Water moderately, allowing the soil to dry between watering.

Deadhead spent flowers to encourage more blooms.
